First of all, I would like to wish you a belated happy new years. In the last news post from about six months ago, I said “I'm finally on break again soon, so I have a handful of stuff planned for the site in the coming months.” Unfortunately, I was pretty lazy during that break and pretty much got nothing new done on the site. Any sort of development around it had gone completely stagnant, and I want to apologize for not fulfilling any of the promises I made about updating the site.


Right after that, my Fall semester started and I got super busy with work (and procrastinating). Around the end of the semester, I had finally started working on my own imageboard software, Kōshi, from scratch for hikari3. The main reason I wanted to start from scratch instead of forking LynxChan (which is what we’ve been using) is because I wanted to make some super significant changes to how the site worked, and I thought it’d just be easier for me to just start from scratch. Also I just thought it’d be a neat project to do to get some development experience (I have almost none outside of hikari3). Most of the features and way the site works should stay relatively the same on the user’s end. Following it's debut on hikari3, Kōshi will be come fully open source and available for contributions from the community to make it better, and forks/clones to use it in your own site and change it as you see fit.


Along with the rewrite comes new features, layouts, bug fixes, and some other stuff. I want it to be known that some of the currently known bugs on the current iteration of the site won’t be fixed until the new site, so be patient please. Obviously, with something completely new, there’s probably going to be tons of bugs that we’ll need to work out, but from what we have so far, it’s works pretty well (albeit with some shit code).


If you have any suggestions or feature requests for the new software, feel free to make a post in the new thread in /h3/

USER BOARD CREATION DISABLED by syrno - 7/21/23 @ 8:22 PM (UTC) #

As most of you know, we've had user board creation enabled. Starting soon now, we will no longer have this feature open.

BUT this does not mean we are getting rid of the idea. This just means that instead of boards being made by users directly, they will be requested instead. There will be a dedicated thread for board creation requests, where you will answer several questions about your requested board so that I know you will put proper effort into managing your board.

Why? Easier and better quality control. I don't want an abundance of boards with no posts. It would make the site feel empty.

I feel like this change would be better for our much smaller community.

As you may have noticed, /a/ and /jp/ were merged into one board, since both are on similar topics and fits better with our small user-base. Sorry if you liked this feature from before, but I feel like this would be more beneficial for a smaller community.
